This issue is not limited to Meta alone. Many technology companies, including Apple and OpenAI, rely on user data to develop and improve new AI
systems. In a competitive digital market, companies feel pressured to adopt advanced data-driven technologies in order to survive. If one company
introduces a powerful AI system, others are likely to follow sooner or later. A well-known example is Nokia, which failed to adapt to the rise of
Android smartphones and eventually lost its position in the market. Similarly, today’s technology companies treat data collection and AI training as
necessary for survival, even when it raises serious concerns about privacy and user control.

This raises an important question: are we blindly following AI? In many ways, the answer appears to be yes. Even though AI technology seems highly
engaging and dopamine-boosting, it is still harmful. The future is even more dangerous, as AI profiling and data tracking are expected to grow even
further, making the future of digital privacy more concerning. In this context, it is important to mention Meta’s recent innovation known as Ray-
Ban Meta Smart Glasses. These smart glasses combine eyewear with built-in AI and allow users to take photos, record audio, and interact with Meta
AI through voice commands. However, these glasses raise serious privacy concerns. They can easily record audio and video of people nearby without
their knowledge. Although there is a small LED light that shows when the camera is on, many critics say it is too easy to miss, meaning most people may
not realize they are being recorded. The concern is not only about recording, but also about how this data can be sent to AI systems, then analyse
conversations, recognise objects, and link information to online data. These concerns are especially significant in the European Union, where stricter
rules apply to the GDPR. Given Meta’s past privacy violations, the introduction of such technologies raises serious questions about how far data-driven
AI systems will go and what this means for future privacy protection.

4. Conclusion

To sum up the questions introduced to the beginning of the essay, the evidence and arguments presented in this essay address them directly.

From the analysis, it is clear that Meta’s AI-driven data tracking and profiling practices significantly threaten online privacy by reducing users’
awareness and control over how their personal data is collected and analyses. Advanced profiling tools operate largely in the background, making it
difficult for individuals to understand or meaningfully influence their online data trail.

As for the second question, the analysis of EU data protection laws shows that frameworks such as the GDPR and the Digital Markets Act do place
important legal limits on these practices through strict consent requirements, transparency obligations, and substantial financial penalties. However,
while these laws offer a level of protection and have forced companies to adjust some of their data strategies, user control remains limited and
fragile in the face of increasingly powerful AI profiling technologies. In my opinion, companies should be transparent about which user
data they collect, where and why this data is used, how long it is stored, and how it is processed. In addition, since European laws are
different, companies should adapt their platforms to follow each country’s laws. Beyond this, there should be new regulations that
clearly define which user data companies are allowed to use for their AI systems or algorithms. These rules should exist outside of
company policies and be controlled by law, so that violations can be properly identified and punished. It is clear that privacy is becoming
weaker day by day due to the rapid use of AI. Law alone cannot fully stop this trend unless users from all countries are willing to take
action and demand stronger protection. However, as we all know, most users are unlikely to take such action or stop using these
technologies, as they continue to enjoy the benefits of AI while neglecting how their data is being stolen and used.
